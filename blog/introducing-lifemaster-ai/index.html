Introducing LifeMaster AI — Your Personal Discipline OS

By Kive Apps April 1, 2026 9 min read

TL;DR

  • LifeMaster AI is our first flagship app.
  • It is a completely offline discipline OS.
  • No ads, no tracking, pure focus.

For the last several months we've been heads-down building something we wish had existed years ago: an Android app that treats your discipline like an operating system — not a checklist, not a habit tracker, but a layered system of tools that work together to help you change.

Today we're shipping it. LifeMaster AI is live on Google Play. It's free, it's offline, and it ships with zero data collection.

This post explains why we built it, what's actually inside, and what you can expect from us going forward.

Why we built it

The honest version is that we got tired of being a target.

Every productivity app we tried felt like a Trojan horse. The good ones were either expensive subscription products that gated essential features behind paywalls, or "free" apps that were paid for by you — in the form of behavioral data, ad inventory, and constant attention extraction. Most of them were sticky for the wrong reasons. They didn't help you build discipline. They built dependence.

A tool that promises to fix your focus shouldn't break it in the process.

We wanted something that worked the way we wished apps worked: respecting your time, not consuming it. Quiet by default. Powerful when you opened it. Useless when you didn't need it.

What's actually inside

LifeMaster AI is built around four pillars. Each one is a self-contained feature, but they share data so the experience compounds.

1. Focus

A clean focus timer with deep-work blocks, a distraction journal you can dump thoughts into without breaking flow, and an end-of-session reflection prompt. No streaks. No leaderboards. No "you've earned 12 trees" gamification. Just the work, and a quiet record that you did it.

2. Recovery

Sleep windows, energy check-ins, and a guided wind-down sequence. The recovery module is intentionally low-pressure — we believe most apps push too hard on recovery and turn rest into another performance metric. Here, "I'm tired" is a complete answer.

3. Growth

Daily and weekly journals, a coaching tip rotation, and a goals layer that supports both outcome goals (lose 5kg) and process goals (write 500 words a day). The coaching tips are small, contextual, and rotate based on what you're working on — not a generic "drink water" reminder.

4. Discipline

Challenges (30-day cold shower, 21-day no-phone-in-bed, etc.), commitment contracts you write to yourself, and a "future-self letter" feature where today-you writes to next-week-you. Discipline isn't grit; it's pre-commitment. The app makes pre-commitment easier.

What we deliberately didn't build

This is almost more important than what we did build. Things you won't find:

  • No accounts. Open the app and start. We don't know who you are.
  • No cloud sync in v1. Your data lives on your device. We're shipping device-to-device export soon, but never to our servers.
  • No social features. No followers, no leaderboards, no "your friend just completed a 7-day streak." Discipline is private.
  • No notifications spam. One opt-in morning prompt, one optional evening prompt. That's it.
  • No subscription. The full app is free. There's a "Buy Us a Chai" option if you want to support us, but every feature works for everyone.
  • No analytics. Not Firebase, not Mixpanel, not anonymized "telemetry". Nothing. We don't know how many of you opened the app today, and that's fine.
Our promise LifeMaster AI is free forever. No ads, no tracking, no upsells. The "Buy Us a Chai" tip jar is the only revenue source, and it's strictly optional.

How it actually feels to use

If you opened a typical productivity app today, you'd be greeted by an onboarding gauntlet: ten screens, four permission requests, an account form, and a free-trial paywall.

LifeMaster AI opens to a single screen with three soft prompts:

  1. What's on your mind today?
  2. What's one thing you want to do?
  3. What's one thing you want to avoid?

You can skip all three. The app remembers nothing if you don't tell it to. The features unfold as you use them. There's no tutorial — everything is labeled, and every screen has a small "?" button that explains what the screen does in two sentences.

Behind the scenes: how we made it free and private

People ask how a free app with no ads and no tracking pays for itself. Honest answer: it doesn't, yet. We have day jobs. The "Buy Us a Chai" donations cover hosting (which is essentially $0 because we use static GitHub Pages and an encrypted-static API system) and a small annual budget for Play Store fees, design tools, and the occasional new test device.

Long-term sustainability comes from two places: optional power-user features that we may charge for someday (think very specific niche tools, not core functionality), and the fact that simple, well-built software has very low maintenance cost.

We are deliberately not optimizing for growth. We'd rather have ten thousand happy long-term users than a million users who churn after a week.

What's next

v1.0 is a foundation, not a finish line. On the roadmap:

  • Device-to-device export — back up your data with a QR code, restore it on a new phone, never touch our servers.
  • Voice journaling — transcribed locally on the device using on-device speech recognition.
  • Widgets — a single-tap focus timer and a "what's one thing today?" widget for the home screen.
  • Localization — Hindi first, then a few major languages.
  • Tablet layout — the app is currently phone-first; tablet polish is next.

Try it, then tell us what's wrong

The fastest way to make this app better is to use it for a week and email us about what felt off. Every reply gets read by an actual human who will probably implement your suggestion if it's reasonable.

You can grab LifeMaster AI on the Google Play Store, and you can email us anytime at kive.apps@gmail.com.

Thanks for being here. The next post will be a deep dive into how we designed the coaching engine to feel "AI" without ever sending your data to the cloud.

Want to support us without spending a rupee? Tell one friend who needs it. That's worth more than any in-app purchase.
